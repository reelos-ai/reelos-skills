## Hybrid Rules

- Start with typography: if font voices conflict, the visual system will not hold.
- Combine **structure** from one system with **material/color** from another; do not combine motifs from many systems.
- Chinese + Western works best as Chinese material/rhythm plus Western grid, or Western clarity plus Chinese accent/material.
- Zen + Sci-fi works best as void, breath, telemetry, and restraint; avoid neon cyberpunk.
- Frontier Tech + Western works naturally through grid, mono metadata, and dark editorial hierarchy.
- Fluid Infrastructure + Western works naturally through product sans typography, glass panels, data stats, and restrained cyan/teal motion.
- Fluid Infrastructure + Sci-fi works only when telemetry supports the product story; avoid random neon, excessive terminal tropes, or spaceship dashboards.
- Sonic Creator Studio + Frontier Tech works through creator tooling, AI badges, waveform controls, and blue signal accents; keep the mood musical rather than enterprise.
- Sonic Creator Studio + Sci-fi should feel like a production console or nightclub studio, not a spaceship dashboard.
- Performance Creative Agency + Western works through grid, proof metrics, case studies, and operational clarity.
- Performance Creative Agency + Sci-fi works only as a growth-engine metaphor; keep the conversion story stronger than the cosmic atmosphere.
- Brush Humanity + Chinese is natural; brush fonts must remain accents.
- Fantasy Zen + Japanese/Zen is natural; keep saturation low and motion slow.
- If more than two systems are requested, choose one as dominant and name the others as supporting signals.

## Chinese Design Aesthetics

Core idea: "看不见龙纹，但知道这是中国的" - cultural identity through qi, space, material, and rhythm rather than obvious symbols.

Principles:
- **留白 / 计白当黑**: empty space has visual weight. Use sparse-to-dense contrast to create hierarchy.
- **气韵生动**: design should feel alive through rhythm, brush-like motion, and controlled asymmetry.
- **五行色**: color has cultural weight; use restrained accents.
- **书法线质**: main actions can feel full and centered; dividers/borders can be sharp; textures can use dry-brush imperfection.
- **宋式克制**: single-tone elegance, tiny radii, 1px borders, precision, and quiet luxury.
- **园林空间**: use borrowed views, framed content, progressive reveal, and one focal scene per scroll.
- **水墨晕染**: gradients and motion should feel like controlled uncertainty, not mechanical decoration.
- **材质可触摸**: paper fiber, silk glow, porcelain glaze, ink grain.

Palette anchors:
- Void/ink: `#0A0A0A`, `#141414`, `#1A1A1A`, `#2A2A2A`
- Rice/paper: `#F0EDE8`, `#F5F5F0`, `#E8E0D0`
- Cinnabar: `#C9372C`, hover `#B03025`
- Ochre/gold: `#D4A574`, `#C9A84C`
- Teal/stone green: `#7BA7A6`, `#5A8A6A`

Chinese sub-directions:
- **墨色人文**: black base, cinnabar accent, paper texture, heavy Song type. Use for culture, publishing, art.
- **禅意寂照**: stone, mist blue, moon white, extreme whitespace. Use for meditation, tea, healing, hot springs.
- **新中式商业**: rice white, cinnabar, muted gold, orderly grid, clear hierarchy. Use for premium commerce, finance, hotels, festivals.
- **青绿山水**: deep ink, mineral blue/green, layered mist, scrolling landscape depth. Use for tourism, ecology, exhibitions.

Chinese mechanism map:
- **留白** creates authority by letting important content breathe.
- **五行 / 正色** makes color feel culturally weighted; accents should be few and meaningful.
- **书法线质** maps to UI roles: full strokes for primary actions, sharp side strokes for dividers, dry brush for texture/noise.
- **宋式** means precision: single-color restraint, 1px borders, 2-4px radius, quiet material.
- **园林** means sequenced disclosure: borrowed views, framed scenes, winding routes, one focus per viewport.
- **水墨** means controlled uncertainty: gradients, particles, and reveals should feel organic but directed.
- **时间** means brush rhythm: slow start, confident middle, quiet end.
- **材质** gives the digital surface a body: paper, silk, porcelain, inkstone.

Implementation notes:
- Module spacing: 120-160px for page-level rhythm.
- Rounded corners: 0-4px.
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1)`.
- Entry motion: 0.8-1.2s, stagger around 0.15s.

## Japanese Design Aesthetics

Core idea: beauty in imperfection, pause, impermanence, and flexible boundaries.

Principles:
- **侘寂**: rough, simple, incomplete, time-worn, asymmetrical.
- **間 / Ma**: space, pause, relational distance. Silence is content.
- **物哀**: make moments feel precious because they pass.
- **枯山水**: abstract nature rather than literal nature.
- **和纸**: surfaces as filters for light and air.
- **渋い**: first impression is quiet; depth appears slowly.
- **襖**: boundaries can slide, veil, and reveal.
- **一期一会**: each interaction deserves attention.

Palette anchors:
- Paper/warm light: `#F5F1EB`, `#EBE5DB`
- Ink/charcoal: `#2C2C2C`, `#4A4A4A`
- Dust/stone: `#8A8A8A`, `#C8C4BC`
- Muted accents: indigo `#4A6FA5`, red `#B53B2E`, moss `#6B8F71`, tan `#C4A77D`

Use subtle texture, low contrast, natural transitions, sliding panels, translucent dividers, and non-centered balance.

## Zen / Meditation Aesthetics

Core idea: the design should make the viewer stop. It is not generic minimalism; it is inner quiet.

Principles:
- **留白即内容**: emptiness is the room where meaning appears.
- **不对称平衡**: avoid mechanical symmetry.
- **材质诚实**: paper, ink, stone, clay, water.
- **时间痕迹**: faded edges, patina, sedimented color.
- **单一焦点**: one visual center per screen.
- **慢速动效**: 600-1200ms transitions; breath cycles can be 4-8s.

Palette anchors:
- Rice paper: `#F4F1EA`
- Charcoal text: `#3D3833`
- Deep ink: `#1A1714`, `#2B2825`
- Bamboo/moss: `#5A6B4E`
- Clay: `#A67C5B`
- Water grey: `#6B7D85`

Typography: serif Chinese for titles/body when possible, sans for UI labels. Line-height 1.8-2.0.

Zen execution details:
- Paper base should be warm, e.g. `#F4F1EA`, not cold white.
- Body text should use charcoal such as `#3D3833`, not pure black.
- Motion should feel like ripples: 600-1200ms for UI transitions, 4-8s for breathing loops.
- Texture should be nearly subliminal: paper grain, ink dot, stone roughness.
- A screen with two equal focal points is usually too busy for meditation.

## Western / Swiss / Bauhaus Aesthetics

Core idea: rational clarity can be warm because it removes cognitive friction.

Principles:
- **Geometry**: circle as unity, square as order, triangle as direction.
- **Grid**: 8pt spacing, 12 columns, baseline alignment, mathematical proportions.
- **Typography as design**: type is not decoration; it is hierarchy.
- **Form follows function**: every element must justify itself.
- **Swiss precision**: objective imagery, asymmetrical balance, strict reading order.
- **Material honesty**: do not fake paper/glass/wood unless the concept requires it.
- **Digital minimalism**: content is the interface.

Palette anchors:
- Neutral scale: `#0A0A0A`, `#1A1A1A`, `#2D2D2D`, `#888888`, `#E5E5E5`, `#FFFFFF`
- Functional accents: blue `#2563EB`, red `#DC2626`, amber `#D97706`, green `#059669`

Use for SaaS, product dashboards, technical documentation, editorial systems, and any brief where clarity outranks atmosphere.

## Nordic Aesthetics

Core idea: function, warmth, natural light, and honest materials.

Principles:
- **Function first**: remove useless ornament.
- **Honest materials**: wood, stone, textile, snow, water.
- **Lagom**: not too much, not too little.
- **Hygge**: minimal but warm.
- **Light as design**: bright surfaces and soft shadows.
- **Nature cohabitation**: blur inside/outside.

Palette anchors:
- Snow: `#F5F5F5`
- Glacier/fog: `#E8ECEF`, `#D0D8DC`
- Fjord: `#4A6B7A`
- Forest: `#2E5C3F`
- Wood: `#8B6914`
- Charcoal: `#36454F`, `#1A1F23`

Use for home, architecture, wellness, coffee/book spaces, and quiet technology.

## Dunhuang Aesthetics

Core idea: mineral color, sacred depth, and Silk Road fantasy without kitsch.

Principles:
- Use mineral pigment palettes, dark cave backgrounds, luminous details, flying ribbons, lotus, and caisson geometry.
- Ornament should feel like structure or ritual, not pasted decoration.
- Best for cultural tourism, museums, luxury craft, exhibitions, film/game art direction.

Palette anchors:
- Cave dark: `#1A1410`, `#2A1F18`
- Ochre: `#B87333`
- Mineral teal: `#2E8B8B`
- Cinnabar: `#C9372C`
- Gold: `#D4A574`
- Wall base: `#E8DCC4`

## Indian Aesthetics

Principles:

- Best for fashion, textile, food/spice, yoga, music, festivals.

## African Aesthetics

Core idea: rhythm, earth, body, ancestry, and symbolic pattern.

Principles:
- Use repeated geometry, large rhythm, earth color, craft texture, bead/grid patterns, and embodied motion.
- Avoid treating tribal motifs as generic decoration; make pattern carry rhythm or storytelling.
- Best for music, fashion, travel, craft, social enterprise, and ecological hospitality.

Palette anchors:

- Red soil: `#C4713B`
- Sunset: `#FF8C00`

## Sci-Fi / Deep Space Aesthetics

Core idea: signal in the void. Make data feel discovered, not decorated.

Principles:
- Use telemetry, coordinates, waveform panels, confidence metrics, timelines, narrow bands, and decoded-message moments.
- Use narrative artifacts: signal age, bandwidth, origin, confidence, chronology, decoded verse.
- Favor monospaced type for data, display type for system names, serif for decoded poetic content, and Chinese serif for solemn translated fragments.
- Pair bioluminescent cyan with amber signal highlights; keep the rest dark and atmospheric.
- Motion can scan, pulse, flicker, or decode; keep it purposeful.

Palette anchors:
- Void: `#030508`, `#0A0F1A`
- Panel: `#101825`, `#1A2438`, `#243050`
- Signal cyan: `#00E5C0`
- Deep teal: `#006B5A`
- Warning gold: `#FFD166`
- Text: `#E0E6F0`, `#8A95A8`

Deep space pattern:
- Background layers: void `#030508`, deep `#0A0F1A`, panel `#101825`, nebula `#243050`.
- Primary signal: cyan `#00E5C0`; secondary signal: amber `#FFD166`.
- UI structure: first declare the signal, then quantify it, then decode it, then place it in time.
- Avoid generic spaceship panels. The design should feel like evidence crossing silence.

## Fluid Infrastructure Aesthetics

Core idea: infrastructure as a living liquid system. The design should feel adaptive, observable, and resilient, not like decorative cyberpunk.

Principles:
- **Liquid depth**: use WebGL, canvas, radial gradients, or large blurred light fields to imply flow behind the interface.
- **Glass control surfaces**: panels should feel like dark translucent instrumentation, with low-alpha white borders and controlled blur.
- **Cyan/teal signal**: use cyan and teal as active system energy; keep large surfaces near-black.
- **Adaptive motion**: orbs, nodes, and cards can float on slow staggered loops. Motion should feel like pressure redistribution, not bouncing decoration.
- **Data as proof**: latency, regions, uptime, deploys, and flow steps make the atmosphere credible.
- **Typography restraint**: neutral sans, medium weights, uppercase metadata, and tight English display type keep the system premium.

Palette anchors:
- Void: `#000000`, `#050606`, `#070A0A`
- Glass surface: `rgba(0,0,0,0.40-0.80)`, `rgba(255,255,255,0.03-0.06)`
- Border: `rgba(255,255,255,0.05-0.15)`
- Primary cyan: `#06B6D4`
- Electric cyan: `#22D3EE`
- Teal: `#14B8A6`, `#2DD4BF`
- Text: `#FFFFFF`, muted `rgba(255,255,255,0.55-0.76)`

Aura Cloud pattern:
- Background: near-black base with oversized blurred cyan/teal blobs around `10%` alpha and blur around `120-160px`.
- Hero layer: one full-bleed shader/canvas or subtle liquid gradient; keep it behind text and below navigation.
- Panels: black at `40-60%` opacity, `backdrop-blur-md/xl`, 1px low-alpha white borders, soft rounded containers.
- Motion: floating modules use 6-9s `ease-in-out` loops with different delays; hover states stay within `scale(1.05-1.1)` and `translateY(-1px to -4px)`.
- Buttons: reserve cyan/teal gradient border and glow for the primary CTA; secondary actions stay glassy and quiet.
- Avoid: neon flooding, high-frequency flicker, random terminal fonts, pure blue-purple gradients, or motion unrelated to system behavior.

## Sonic Creator Studio Aesthetics

Core idea: a black-stage AI music studio where sound becomes interface. The design should feel like creator tooling, track discovery, and live production, not a generic neon SaaS page.

Principles:
- **Black stage first**: use true black and slate-black as the performance space; let blue light behave like sound energy.
- **Waveform language**: icons, audio bars, progress tracks, orbiting discs, and waveform cards should carry the concept.
- **Creator-safe credibility**: show licensing, BPM, artist, stems, export, DAW integration, and commercial use details.
- **Blue pulse as audio signal**: blue gradients and glow can highlight playback, generation, and active tracks; keep them away from long text.
- **Album/card imagery**: track cards need real visual art or cover-like assets; purely abstract backgrounds feel thin for music.
- **Motion as sound**: use orbit, scan, pulse, and bar bounce; avoid random particle storms that do not map to music behavior.

Palette anchors:
- Stage black: `#000000`, `#020617`, `#030712`
- Slate panels: `#0F172A`, `#111827`, `#1E293B`
- Primary blue: `#3B82F6`
- Electric blue: `#60A5FA`
- Deep blue: `#1D4ED8`, `#2563EB`
- Cyan glow: `#22D3EE`, `rgba(34,211,238,0.2-0.6)`
- Text: `#F8FAFC`, muted `#CBD5E1`, `#94A3B8`
- Border/surface: `rgba(255,255,255,0.05-0.15)`

NovaPulse pattern:
- Hero: black background, Manrope headline, one blue gradient phrase, and a product preview that resembles a music player or orbiting record.
- Header: sticky black/slate gradient with backdrop blur and thin white border.
- CTA: dark pill with blue/conic border or blue fill; secondary CTA stays transparent with a thin border.
- Player surface: slate-to-black gradient, rounded 24-26px, blue border at 20-50% alpha, cyan glow shadow.
- Track cards: image-led cards with hover scale around `1.05`, blue gradient overlays, artist/BPM metadata.
- Motion: 20s linear orbit for record/ring elements, 3s glow pulse, 2.5s scan line, 1s audio-bar bounce, 0.6-0.8s enter-up reveal.
- Avoid: purple-pink EDM overload, fake equalizer decoration everywhere, illegible glowing text, and overusing mono fonts for music metadata.

## Performance Creative Agency Aesthetics

Core idea: creative output as a growth engine. The design should feel like a high-throughput ad studio that measures, ships, and iterates, not a decorative agency portfolio.

Principles:
- **Proof before ornament**: ROAS lift, brands scaled, turnaround, variations per cycle, creators, retention, and ad spend must anchor the narrative.
- **Operational interface**: kanban boards, task rows, dates, status chips, inclusions tables, process steps, and dashboards make the agency feel executable.
- **Black engine atmosphere**: use a dark base with luminous light-trail or gravity-field imagery to suggest speed, motion, and compounding performance.
- **Pixel display restraint**: pixel/gothic display type can make the brand memorable, but reserve it for headlines and metrics.
- **Mono conversion controls**: navigation, CTAs, labels, and task metadata should feel precise and low-friction.
- **Creative specificity**: mention static ads, UGC, hooks, CPA, ROAS, creator networks, and revision cycles; generic "creative excellence" is too soft.

Palette anchors:
- Engine black: `#000000`, `#0B0F18`, `#111827`
- Panel graphite: `#14161C`, `#1C1F26`, `#262A34`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Muted gray: `#9CA3AF`, `#D1D5DB`
- Proof blue: `#2563EB`, `#3B82F6`, `#60A5FA`
- Purple accent: `#A855F7`, `#C084FC`
- Cyan/teal accent: `#22D3EE`, `#2DD4BF`
- Success dot: `#34D399`
- Border/surface: `rgba(255,255,255,0.05-0.20)`

Nexura pattern:
- Hero: full-bleed light-trail / gravity-field background, centered pixel headline, small mono availability chip, and decisive pill CTA.
- Typography: `DotGothic16` for hero, section titles, and stats; mono for nav, CTAs, body, task rows, labels, and proof copy.
- Product proof: place ROAS, brand count, and turnaround above or near the first product/workflow preview.
- Workflow preview: use a dark kanban board or task dashboard with graphite panels, blue primary action, status columns, dates, and assignee avatars.
- Cards: low-alpha gradient borders, graphite fills, 16-24px radius, restrained colored avatar dots only where they identify people/proof.
- Motion: 0.8s reveal from opacity/blur/vertical shift; small hover border brightening; green availability dot can pulse slowly.
- Avoid: generic portfolio masonry, pastel agency friendliness, over-bright gradients, pixel font in paragraphs, and cosmic visuals that are not tied to growth speed.

## Frontier Tech Community / Intelligence Aesthetics

Core idea: a dark editorial interface for people tracking the edge of AI, chips, and frontier technology. It should feel like community, signal, and market intelligence rather than generic cyberpunk.

Principles:
- **Typography leads**: compressed uppercase display for headlines, geometric sans for readable body text, mono for nav/metadata/issue numbers.
- **Dark void plus warm text**: use near-black backgrounds and warm off-white foregrounds instead of pure white.
- **One acidic accent**: lime can carry primary signal, live status, arrows, links, and tiny dots. Do not spread it across large surfaces.
- **Transparent surface system**: cards can use very low alpha white fills and borders, e.g. white at 3-8% fill and 6-12% border.
- **Editorial intelligence layout**: issue cards, section numbers, timestamps, stack layers, event metadata, and manifesto badges make the system feel purposeful.
- **Hero imagery**: real cinematic community or frontier-tech imagery can carry emotion; overlays keep text legible.
- **Motion**: short, precise transitions; small scale on active states; subtle glow only on key status dots.

Palette anchors:
- Background: `#0A0A0A`, `#111111`
- Foreground: `#F5F5F0`
- Muted foreground: `rgba(245,245,240,0.6-0.8)`
- Acid lime: `#BFFF00`
- Warning orange: `#FF6B00`
- Danger red: `#FF6B6B`
- Border/surface: `rgba(255,255,255,0.04-0.12)`

Use for frontier tech communities, AI research/event brands, intelligence newsletters, founder/investor networks, and technical media products.

Avoid:
- Full cyberpunk neon palettes.
- Overusing lime as a background color.
- Terminal cosplay where normal editorial hierarchy would be clearer.
- Display fonts for long paragraphs.

## Brush Humanity / Literary Ink Aesthetics

Core idea: text is the artifact. Poetry, calligraphy, and literary prose create the emotional center; visuals should behave like paper, ink, and hand pressure.

Principles:
- Use serif Chinese as the main voice; use brush fonts only for short seals, tags, or emotional signatures.
- Poetry can become layout: short lines, generous leading, vertical rhythm, and quiet author/date metadata.
- Literary pages need "human breath": line-height around 2, low contrast dividers, warm paper, ink-wash backgrounds.
- Interactive drawing/brush gestures should be slow and tactile, not gamified.
- Imperfection is meaning: dry ink, missing original, copied trace, and repair can become narrative depth.

Palette anchors:
- Ink: `#0A0A0A`, `#1C1C1C`, `#2D2D2D`
- Paper/gold: `#FDFCF7`, `#FAF6ED`, `#F0E8D4`, `#C4A35A`
- Cinnabar: `#B83A2E`, `#D4655A`

Use for poetry, calligraphy, cultural essays, museums, scholar brands, publishing, and humanistic archives.

## Fantasy Zen Aesthetics

Core idea: inner quiet turns into a subtle imaginary cosmos. It is Zen plus dream logic, not fantasy spectacle.

Principles:
- Keep the base dark and quiet; use low-saturation moon, mist, stone, and muted fantasy accents.
- Let particles, dust, and light behave like thoughts appearing during meditation.
- Use poetic sections and sparse maxims; avoid dense explanatory UI.
- Symbols should be abstract: void, flower/world, leaf/universe, sound becoming shape.
- Motion can float and breathe, but should end in stillness.

Palette anchors:
- Void/ink: `#0D0C0B`, `#1A1816`, `#2A2724`
- Mist/moon: `#A39E96`, `#C4BDB3`, `#E2DDD4`, `#F4F1EA`
- Muted fantasy accents: `#8B9DAF`, `#A68B7C`, `#7A8B6E`, `#9B8AA5`

Use for immersive meditation pages, conceptual art, poetic product launches, retreats, and calm speculative experiences.
