# Onion Reading System

Use this reference when the user wants to optimize the reading method itself, build a reusable book-processing pipeline, or turn reading into output.

## Core Thesis

Reading is learning within limited usable time at the level the reader can understand. The goal is not self-satisfaction; it is to change cognition and action.

Three base abilities:

- Search well.
- Keep curiosity active.
- Think independently.

## Mindset For Fast Reading

1. Drop excessive reverence for books. Every book has context and limits; not understanding a book is not always the reader's fault.
2. Some books only need fast reading. Use the 80/20 rule to find maximum value with minimum time.
3. Even books worth deep reading should be skimmed first. Skimming creates questions and curiosity.

Default sequence:

```text
评测 -> 速读 -> 休息 -> 精读 -> 强制输出
```

## Evaluation Before Reading

Goal: decide whether the book is worth reading and create desire to read.

Inputs:

- Preface
- Table of contents
- Afterword
- Reviews and short comments
- Author background and social context

Rules:

- Read reviews skeptically.
- Good reviews restore the book to its social and intellectual context.
- Short comments can be useful when they trigger a sharp question.

Output:

- Why read this book?
- What problem should it solve?
- What questions do I want the book to answer?

## Fast Reading: Find Key Information

Fast reading means finding semantic units quickly, accurately, and stably, while skipping support material.

Use visual input instead of subvocal linear input.

Look for:

- First and last sentences.
- Transition words: but, however, yet, although.
- Nouns and verbs.
- Summary paragraphs near the end.
- New, upgraded, or replacement concepts.

Ask while skimming:

- What problem is the author addressing?
- What claim is the author making?
- What evidence supports it?
- Which concepts are new, newly important, or meant to replace an old belief?

## 因概少切复

Fast reading flow:

1. 因: 提问, 5 min. Ask why this book matters, what similar books are known, and what open questions the book should answer.
2. 概: 预习, 15 min. Read cover, preface, table of contents, afterword, blurbs. Decide whether it is simple-worth-reading, hard-but-worth-reading, or not worth reading.
3. 少: 扫读, 10 min. Flip through the whole book; mark keywords, headings, diagrams, first/last paragraphs.
4. 切: 切重点, 45 min. Use the nine-grid note to isolate concepts, cases, aha moments, next actions.
5. 复: 复习, 30 min. Ask what remains unclear, what books to read next, and what action follows.

Suggested daily rhythm:

- Morning: questions + preview.
- Noon: skim.
- Evening: cut key points.
- Next morning: review.

Keep reading time within about 2 hours when possible; reserve time for thinking and work.

## Nine-Grid Note

Use this as the default extraction table for fast reading:

```text
书名 | 问题 | 阿哈
概念 | 核心 | 案例
书单延伸 | 感悟 | 接下来做
```

## Fragment Reading: IPO

Fragment reading is purposeful extraction from fragmented information.

```text
找到内容 -> 提炼 -> 链接 -> 融合
```

- 提炼: restate in your own words; turn information into concepts.
- 链接: connect with personal experience and prior knowledge.
- 融合: create your own output and share it.

## Cards And Notes

Notes are knowledge crystals, not copied fragments.

Flow:

```text
标记 -> 收集 -> 整理 -> 输出 -> 应用
```

Rules:

- One card equals one concept.
- Use your own words.
- Build concept cards, quote cards, story cards, and method cards.
- 33 solid cards can become a course, essay series, or small book.

## Thematic Reading

Goal: build a domain knowledge system.

Three ammunition types:

- 碎弹: fragment-based thematic reading around one theme.
- 爆破弹: book-based thematic reading, usually 3 books on one theme.
- 核弹: long-term deep research over years.

Three steps:

```text
提出问题 -> 建立框架 -> 寻找答案
```

Pick 3 important books per field when starting a new domain.

## Deep Reading: 练兵场

Goal: upgrade the angle of seeing a problem and extract underlying laws.

- 练: find connections through analogy and visualization.
- 兵: explore the iceberg; concepts are visible, theories and laws are below the surface.
- 场: test the theory across multiple scenarios.

Tools:

- Summary writing
- Feynman technique
- Analytical reading: cause, effect, boundary
- Distributed rereading
- Surround-point support: start with accessible books before big books

## Output And Teaching

Effective reading requires output. Output forces clarity.

Use:

- Writing for logic.
- Oral explanation for story and imagery.
- Teaching to learn.
- Keyword cards to visualize the talk.

Input-output ratio guideline: 10:1.

Build:

- Quote library
- Concept library
- Story library

## Reading-To-Action Loop

```text
封面/序/尾 -> 写 3 个问题
带问题阅读
读后回答 3 个问题
写下行动目标
分析目标与现实差距
细化成 MVP
行动中迭代
```

Rule: completion beats perfection. There is no good or bad, only whether you act.
