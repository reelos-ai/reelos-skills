# Product-promo story arcs

Select one arc after research. Keep every claim source-backed.

## Problem → mechanism → proof

Use for analytical tools, developer products, infrastructure, and complex SaaS.

1. Show the costly old behavior.
2. Reveal the product.
3. Demonstrate the underlying workflow.
4. Show evidence, verification, or guardrails.
5. End with a concrete action.

## Before → after → how

Use when the product has an obvious visual transformation.

1. Show the fragmented state.
2. Show the unified outcome.
3. Explain the two or three actions that create it.
4. Confirm the user benefit.

## Moment-of-use

Use when the product supports a repeated daily workflow.

1. Enter at the moment of tension.
2. Follow the user through the workflow.
3. Show how the product changes the next decision.
4. Close on the next natural action.

## Suggested time allocation

- Hook: 0–15%
- Product reveal: 15–25%
- Capabilities and mechanism: 25–65%
- Verification or outcome: 65–85%
- CTA: 85–96%
- Disclaimer: final 4–8%

Treat these percentages as a starting point, not fixed timing.
