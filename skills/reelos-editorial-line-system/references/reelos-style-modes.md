# ReelOS Style Modes

Use this reference when the user asks for ReelOS taste, palette modes, aesthetic variants, or a stronger visual direction. These modes adapt ideas from `reelos-design-taste` into the stricter Editorial Line System: typography-first, black-and-white line art, sparse accents, and clear causal visual storytelling.

Choose one dominant mode. A mode is a skin over the same editorial line-art engine; it should not replace the core monochrome character style.

Default mode: **ReelOS Lab Orbit**. It is the representative ReelOS style and should be the first choice when the user does not specify a mode.

## Mode Selection

| Mode | Best for | Visual logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ReelOS Lab Orbit | personal AI OS, builder labs, AI-native essays, one-person company systems | warm paper plus dark lab, orbital signal, personal system telemetry |
| Ink Editorial | cultural essays, reflective analysis, humanistic tech, publishing | paper, ink, cinnabar, literary breath |
| Modern Tech Editorial | product launches, AI apps, SaaS, model releases, startup analysis | crisp white/graphite, blue signal, product-system clarity |
| Academic Paper | papers, methods, benchmarks, technical explainers, research notes | arXiv paper, equations, diagrams, evidence hierarchy |
| Research Lab Whitepaper | institutional AI research, policy, strategy, whitepapers | sober report grid, citations, evidence boxes, restrained authority |
| Blueprint System | architectures, workflows, systems, mechanisms, infrastructure maps | blueprint field, measured lines, components, assembly logic |
| Data Newspaper | market shifts, rankings, public data, timelines, macro explainers | newsprint grid, charts, numbered evidence, editorial urgency |
| Minimal Product Memo | founder notes, product strategy, internal memos, decision records | sparse memo, product sketch, decision-first hierarchy |
| Frontier Intelligence | AI, chips, policy, investor/community intelligence, technical media | dark editorial signal, mono metadata, acid accent |
| Fluid Infrastructure | cloud, AI infra, observability, deployment, systems | living infrastructure, routing energy, glass control surfaces |
| Sonic Creator Studio | music, creator tools, audio AI, cultural tech | black stage, waveform rhythm, blue playback signal |
| Performance Engine | growth, ad creative, e-commerce ops, high-throughput teams | operational proof, kanban logic, pixel/mono tension |

## Shared Taste Rules

- Start with typography and hierarchy before color.
- Name one dominant mode; allow at most 1-2 supporting signals.
- Keep Chinese letter spacing at `0`; use weight, line-height, and spacing for hierarchy.
- Use accents as semantic signals, not surface decoration.
- Preserve the core line-art characters as mostly black-and-white.
- Use real causal structure for analysis images: conflict, bottleneck, consequence.

## Ink Editorial

Use when the work needs warmth, memory, culture, reflection, or quiet authority.

Typography:
- Display: CJK serif such as Noto Serif SC / Source Han Serif SC.
- Body/UI: Noto Sans SC / Source Han Sans SC for clarity.
- Accent: brush or seal-like text only for short tags, never paragraphs.
- Rhythm: Chinese editorial line-height around `1.8-2.2`.

Palette:
- Ink: `#0A0A0A`, `#1C1C1C`
- Paper: `#FDFCF7`, `#FAF6ED`, `#F0E8D4`
- Cinnabar: `#B83A2E`, `#C9372C`
- Quiet gold: `#C4A35A`, `#D4A574`
- Stone green optional: `#7BA7A6`

Image direction:
- Cream paper background, black line art, large quiet margins, cinnabar stamps or section marks.
- Use paper fiber, ink pressure, reading rooms, notebooks, archives, or framed scenes.
- Avoid dragons, lantern overload, fake antique texture, and brush fonts as body text.

## ReelOS Lab Orbit

Use when the work should feel like ReelOS.ai: an AI-native builder lab where scattered inputs become a personal operating system. This mode sits between Ink Editorial, Frontier Intelligence, and Fluid Infrastructure: warmer than a dark intelligence dashboard, more personal than cloud infra, sharper than a pure literary essay.

Typography:
- Display: Sora / Space Grotesk / IBM Plex Sans for Latin; strong PingFang SC / Noto Sans SC for Chinese headlines.
- Article voice: Songti SC / Noto Serif CJK SC for long reflective Chinese passages or manifesto-like quotes.
- Body/UI: PingFang SC / Noto Sans SC / IBM Plex Sans.
- Meta/HUD: IBM Plex Mono for dates, modes, system readouts, signal labels, and small telemetry.
- Rhythm: Chinese body line-height around `1.65-1.9`; manifesto fragments can breathe at `1.9-2.15`. Keep Chinese tracking at `0`; use mono tracking only for metadata.

Palette:
- Warm paper: `#fffaf1`, `#fffdf8`, `#f7f7f6`, `#f1f1ef`
- Ink/charcoal: `#111111`, `#171717`, `#090907`
- Dark lab: `#05070a`, `#080d13`, `#151515`
- Orbit orange: `#f97316`
- Amber signal: `#f59e0b`, `#f5c68b`
- System blue: `#5b7cff`
- Violet node: `#8b5cf6`
- Article copper: `#c56d24`
- Dark-mode text: `#f6fbff`, warm active ink `#fffaf1`

Image direction:
- Use a warm paper or near-black lab field with a faint grid, scanline texture, and orbit diagrams.
- Make one central system core: a small glowing nucleus, nested rings, or a scroll-like OS hub.
- Show line-art builder figures collecting signals, judging, building, and compounding loops.
- Use orange for agency/action, blue for system intelligence, amber for insight, violet for experimental nodes.
- Use HUD labels sparingly: `MODE`, `SYSTEM`, `STATUS`, `SIGNAL`, `BUILDING`, `PERSONAL OS`.
- Best compositions: orbit board, lab notebook plus telemetry, signal ribbon, personal OS map, article lead image with a central loop.

Guardrails:
- Do not let gradients become the whole style; the structure is orbit, signal, and personal system.
- Avoid generic SaaS hero cards, fake dashboards, and excessive terminal styling.
- Avoid making everything dark; the ReelOS flavor depends on contrast between warm paper thinking and dark lab execution.
- Keep characters monochrome and human-scale. The system should feel built by one focused person, not a faceless enterprise control room.

## Modern Tech Editorial

Use when the image should feel current, clean, and product-intelligent without becoming dark cyberpunk or generic SaaS. Good for AI product analysis, model launches, startup strategy, platform shifts, and technical business writing.

Typography:
- Display: Space Grotesk / Sora / Inter Tight for Latin; PingFang SC / Noto Sans SC 600-800 for Chinese.
- Body/UI: Inter / IBM Plex Sans / Noto Sans SC.
- Meta: IBM Plex Mono or Space Mono for model versions, dates, release notes, and state labels.
- Rhythm: compact headings, generous section spacing, Chinese tracking `0`, short mono labels with slight uppercase tracking.

Palette:
- Base white: `#F8FAFC`, `#FFFFFF`
- Graphite ink: `#0F172A`, `#111827`
- Line gray: `#E5E7EB`, `#CBD5E1`
- Product blue: `#2563EB`, `#3B82F6`
- Signal cyan: `#06B6D4`
- Action orange: `#F97316`
- Success green: `#10B981`

Image direction:
- Use clean white/graphite panels, thin rules, product schematics, model cards, release gates, and interface fragments.
- Accent blue indicates intelligence or product system; orange indicates change, risk, or migration.
- Best structures: before/after split, product stack, adoption funnel, capability ladder, or platform flow.
- Avoid fake glassmorphism, blue-purple gradient hero cliches, and excessive dashboard cards.

## Academic Paper

Use for research papers, model architecture explainers, benchmark results, methodology posts, and technical notes where credibility depends on evidence and structure.

Typography:
- Display/title: restrained serif or technical sans; for Chinese, Noto Serif SC or Noto Sans SC depending on seriousness.
- Body: serif for paper-like authority, sans for annotations.
- Meta/data: mono for equation labels, benchmark IDs, figure numbers, and references.
- Rhythm: figure-caption logic; labels like `Fig. 1`, `Method`, `Result`, `Ablation`, `Limit`.

Palette:
- Paper: `#FBFAF7`, `#F4F1EA`
- Ink: `#111111`, `#2B2B2B`
- Rule gray: `#D8D3C8`, `#B8B2A8`
- Scholar blue: `#1D4ED8`, `#2563EB`
- Review red: `#B91C1C`
- Highlight yellow: `#FACC15`

Image direction:
- Use paper margins, figure panels, equation fragments, benchmark tables, ablation grids, and small line-art researchers.
- Make the central insight read like a paper figure, not a poster slogan.
- Use callouts for method, result, limitation, and implication.
- Avoid overdecorated lab sci-fi, illegible pseudo-equations, and claiming certainty beyond the source.

## Research Lab Whitepaper

Use for serious reports, policy notes, institutional research, AI governance, market strategy, company analysis, and whitepaper-style explainers.

Typography:
- Display: IBM Plex Sans / Source Sans / Noto Sans SC, sober 600-700.
- Body: readable sans or serif depending on document tone.
- Meta: IBM Plex Mono for report numbers, source tags, dates, and confidence levels.
- Rhythm: clear executive summary hierarchy; strong section numbering.

Palette:
- Report paper: `#F7F5F0`, `#FFFFFF`
- Ink: `#101828`
- Muted slate: `#667085`, `#98A2B3`
- Research navy: `#1E3A8A`
- Evidence blue: `#2563EB`
- Caution amber: `#D97706`
- Risk red: `#DC2626`

Image direction:
- Use executive-summary blocks, evidence boxes, risk matrices, timelines, and stakeholder maps.
- Build around decision logic: issue, evidence, implication, recommendation.
- Accent colors should classify evidence, confidence, risk, and recommendation.
- Avoid playful characters, heavy gradients, or startup-launch energy.

## Blueprint System

Use for architecture diagrams, workflows, mechanism explainers, infrastructure layouts, automation loops, and anything where the point is how parts connect.

Typography:
- Display: technical sans, IBM Plex Sans / Inter / Noto Sans SC.
- Meta: mono for coordinates, component IDs, version labels, arrows, and dimensions.
- Rhythm: measured labels, ruler-like spacing, component naming discipline.

Palette:
- Blueprint dark: `#07111F`, `#0B1B2B`
- Blueprint blue: `#0EA5E9`, `#38BDF8`
- Line cyan: `#67E8F9`
- Paper blueprint: `#EFF6FF`
- Ink navy: `#0F172A`
- Warning orange: `#F97316`

Image direction:
- Use grid paper, measured line drawings, component boxes, arrows, dashed dependencies, and assembly labels.
- Characters should be small operators inspecting or connecting parts, not the main subject.
- Best structures: system map, loop diagram, sequence diagram, layered stack, or failure point map.
- Avoid decorative circuitry, random nodes, and cluttered engineering schematics.

## Data Newspaper

Use for market analysis, industry rankings, economic shifts, timelines, public data, policy changes, and news explainers where evidence needs editorial urgency.

Typography:
- Display: bold editorial serif or condensed sans; Chinese can use Noto Serif SC / Source Han Serif SC for authority.
- Body: readable serif/sans mix.
- Meta/data: mono or narrow sans for dates, ranks, percentages, and source labels.
- Rhythm: newspaper hierarchy: headline, deck, figure, caption, source.

Palette:
- Newsprint: `#F5F1E8`, `#EFE7D8`
- Ink: `#111111`
- Rule gray: `#B8B0A0`
- Alert red: `#C1121F`
- Data blue: `#1D4ED8`
- Highlight yellow: `#F2C94C`

Image direction:
- Use newspaper columns, chart strips, timeline bands, rank tables, and annotated evidence.
- Make the main chart or timeline carry the conclusion.
- Use red for breaking/tension, blue for measured data, yellow for the key highlight.
- Avoid generic newspaper nostalgia, fake headlines, and chart clutter.

## Minimal Product Memo

Use for founder notes, product strategy, internal decisions, roadmap debates, product principles, and concise opinion pieces.

Typography:
- Display: Inter / Geist / IBM Plex Sans; Chinese with PingFang SC / Noto Sans SC.
- Body: calm sans, medium line-height.
- Meta: small mono labels for decision ID, owner, date, status.
- Rhythm: memo title, thesis, three bullets, decision strip.

Palette:
- Warm white: `#FAFAF7`, `#FFFFFF`
- Ink: `#111827`
- Soft gray: `#E5E7EB`, `#6B7280`
- Product blue: `#2563EB`
- Decision green: `#059669`
- Concern orange: `#EA580C`

Image direction:
- Use one product sketch, one decision tree, and one short conclusion strip.
- Let whitespace do more work than panels.
- Best structures: decision fork, tradeoff matrix, principle stack, or product loop.
- Avoid ornamental illustrations, large emotional scenes, and too many labels.

## Frontier Intelligence

Use when the work tracks AI, chips, frontier technology, governance, technical media, or market intelligence.

Typography:
- Display: compressed or bold sans energy; for Chinese, use strong Noto Sans SC weight instead of fake all-caps logic.
- Body/UI: Manrope / Inter / Noto Sans SC.
- Meta: Space Mono or mono-like labels for issue numbers, timestamps, model IDs, coordinates, and evidence tags.

Palette:
- Background: `#0A0A0A`, `#111111`
- Foreground: `#F5F5F0`
- Muted foreground: `rgba(245,245,240,0.6-0.8)`
- Acid lime: `#BFFF00`
- Warning orange: `#FF6B00`
- Surface/border: `rgba(255,255,255,0.04-0.12)`

Image direction:
- Dark editorial grid, warm off-white line art, one acidic signal accent.
- Use issue cards, timelines, evidence strips, access gates, model cards, compute maps, and community scenes.
- Avoid cyberpunk neon flooding, terminal cosplay, and lime as a large background.

## Fluid Infrastructure

Use for cloud platforms, AI infra, deployment engines, observability, GPU marketplaces, routing, and adaptive systems.

Typography:
- Display/body/UI: Inter, Geist, system sans, Noto Sans SC.
- Meta: uppercase sans labels with tracking for English; Chinese tracking stays `0`.
- Mood: product trust plus living-system motion.

Palette:
- Void: `#000000`, `#050606`, `#070A0A`
- Glass surface: `rgba(0,0,0,0.40-0.80)`, `rgba(255,255,255,0.03-0.06)`
- Hairline: `rgba(255,255,255,0.05-0.15)`
- Primary cyan: `#06B6D4`
- Electric cyan: `#22D3EE`
- Teal: `#14B8A6`, `#2DD4BF`
- Text: `#FFFFFF`, muted `rgba(255,255,255,0.55-0.76)`

Image direction:
- Dark system map, line-art operators, routing paths, node diagrams, cloud panels, and compute flows.
- Use cyan/teal only for active paths, latency signals, deployment states, or observability highlights.
- Avoid random neon, fake spaceship dashboards, and motion unrelated to system behavior.

## Sonic Creator Studio

Use for AI music, creator-safe audio, track libraries, sample marketplaces, music tools, and cultural creator products.

Typography:
- Display: Manrope or rounded geometric sans.
- Body/UI: Inter / system sans / Noto Sans SC.
- Metadata: readable sans labels for BPM, license, stems, track state, and export.

Palette:
- Stage black: `#000000`, `#020617`, `#030712`
- Slate panels: `#0F172A`, `#111827`, `#1E293B`
- Primary blue: `#3B82F6`
- Electric blue: `#60A5FA`
- Deep blue: `#1D4ED8`, `#2563EB`
- Cyan glow: `#22D3EE`
- Text: `#F8FAFC`, muted `#CBD5E1`, `#94A3B8`

Image direction:
- Black-stage composition, waveform bars, orbiting record/player modules, track cards, creator controls.
- Keep line-art people monochrome; blue accents indicate playback, generation, or audio energy.
- Avoid purple-pink EDM overload and fake equalizers everywhere.

## Performance Engine

Use for growth agencies, ad creative ops, e-commerce performance, UGC pipelines, and workflow/proof-heavy business visuals.

Typography:
- Display: pixel/grotesque energy only for short titles and metrics.
- Body/UI/meta: mono or mono-like sans for task rows, dates, proof labels, statuses, and CTAs.
- Chinese: use clean Noto Sans SC; simulate operational sharpness with grid, not unreadable pixel CJK.

Palette:
- Engine black: `#000000`, `#0B0F18`, `#111827`
- Panel graphite: `#14161C`, `#1C1F26`, `#262A34`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Muted gray: `#9CA3AF`, `#D1D5DB`
- Proof blue: `#2563EB`, `#3B82F6`, `#60A5FA`
- Purple accent: `#A855F7`, `#C084FC`
- Cyan/teal accent: `#22D3EE`, `#2DD4BF`
- Success dot: `#34D399`

Image direction:
- Operational workflow boards, proof metrics, task rows, conversion controls, iteration loops, throughput lines.
- Use colored accents only for proof, live state, or workflow status.
- Avoid generic portfolio masonry, cheerful pastel agency look, and pixel fonts in paragraphs.
